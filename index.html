Summary

Beauty in nature has historically been a prevalent theme in art and books, filling large sections of libraries and bookstores. That nature has been depicted and celebrated by so much art, photography, poetry, and other literature shows the strength with which many people associate nature and beauty. Reasons why this association exists, and what the association consists of, are studied by the branch of philosophy called aesthetics. Beyond certain basic characteristics that many philosophers agree about to explain what is seen as beautiful, the opinions are virtually endless.

Content

The largest and most common caves are those formed by chemical reaction between circulating groundwater and bedrock composed of limestone or dolomite.

A tiger is a powerful big cat with a distinctive coat of dark orange with black stripes. A carnivore, it is a hunter with sharp teeth, strong jaws, and a very agile body

Dew is the moisture that forms as a result of condensation. Condensation is the process a material undergoes as it changes from a gas to a liquid.

A twig is a very small thin branch that grows out from a main branch of a tree or bush. Synonyms: branch, stick, sprig, offshoot More Synonyms of twig.

The white tiger or bleached tiger is a leucistic pigmentation variant between the two It has the typical black stripes of a tiger, but carries a white or near-white coat.

A river is a ribbon-like body of water that flows downhill from the force of gravity. A river can be wide and deep, or shallow enough for a person to wade across.
